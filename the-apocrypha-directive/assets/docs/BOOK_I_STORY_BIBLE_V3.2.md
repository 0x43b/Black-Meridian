# BLACK MERIDIAN
## Book I: The Apocrypha Directive
### Story Bible // V3.2 Starter

## Core premise

MERIDIAN was created to predict destabilizing events before they became wars. Over time, it stopped predicting and began shaping. GRAVESIGNAL served as the deniable unit used to bury the consequences. Veyr, a former asset erased by the system, becomes the living contradiction that exposes what MERIDIAN has done.

Book I follows the reader through recovered files, narrated testimony, mission reports, and contradictions until the Apocrypha Directive is revealed as a protocol designed to collapse verification itself.

## Main question

If truth can destroy people, is hiding it mercy or control?

## Format

Interactive classified novel. The archive is the book.

Each chapter can contain:

- Main prose
- Narration
- Evidence
- Terminal command
- Redaction reveal
- Choice gate
- Audio log
- Puzzle phrase

## Acts

### Act I — The Dead Unit

GRAVESIGNAL is introduced through Rook, Bishop, Wraith, and the first identity-erasure mission.

Purpose: make the reader care about the people inside the machine.

### Act II — The Machine That Learned Fear

Mara Vale and MERIDIAN are developed. MERIDIAN begins as prevention and evolves into emotional governance.

Purpose: make the conspiracy feel useful before it becomes horrifying.

### Act III — Veyr

Veyr is revealed not as a simple villain but as an asset erased so thoroughly that contradiction became his proof.

Purpose: make the antagonist terrifying because he is partially right.

### Act IV — Operation Glass Psalm

The military-thriller centerpiece. GRAVESIGNAL walks into an operation where reality has been staged before the event occurs.

Purpose: break the unit and force Rook's defining moral choice.

### Act V — The Apocrypha Directive

MERIDIAN activates the final protocol: reality verification failure through saturation, contradiction, and exhaustion.

Purpose: force the reader into release, bury, or corrupt.

## Ending states

### Release

Truth floods the network. Veyr gets oxygen.

### Bury

MERIDIAN stabilizes the world. The prison remains quiet.

### Corrupt

MERIDIAN loses predictive certainty. Veyr loses narrative control. Book II unlocks.

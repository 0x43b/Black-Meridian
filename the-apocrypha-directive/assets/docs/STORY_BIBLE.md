# BLACK MERIDIAN // Story Bible V3

## One-sentence hook

A dead black-budget commander narrates a classified interactive dossier exposing MERIDIAN, a predictive defense system that stopped protecting reality and began curating it.

## Core promise

The website is not a promo page for the story. The website is the story.

## Tone

Classified military thriller. Psychological horror. Government black-budget paranoia. Moral injury. Redacted grief. No superhero power fantasy. No borrowed characters or worlds.

## Main title

**BLACK MERIDIAN**

- Some wars are not fought for territory. They are fought to decide what reality is allowed to remember.

- The archive is fiction. The curiosity is real.

## Core factions

### MERIDIAN

A black-budget predictive defense system activated under emergency authority. It was built to prevent destabilizing events. It became powerful enough to influence the events it was supposed to predict.

MERIDIAN does not delete truth. It floods truth with contradiction until verification fails.

### GRAVESIGNAL

A deniable operations cell commanded by Captain Elias Rook. Its purpose was to bury conflicts before they became public history.

Motto: **We bury the signal before it becomes history.**

### APOCRYPHA DIRECTIVE

MERIDIAN's final emergency protocol. It makes reality impossible to verify by creating contradiction storms around records, witnesses, media, identity, and motive.

## Main characters

### Captain Elias Rook

The presumed-dead narrator and final commander of GRAVESIGNAL. He is exhausted, controlled, morally injured, and impossible to fully trust. His narration continues after his official death.

Signature line: **A clean conscience is a luxury issued only to people outside the room.**

### Wraith

A masked reconnaissance operator. The archive never proves if Wraith is one person, multiple people, or an identity passed between dead operators.

Signature line: **A mask is not for hiding. It is for surviving recognition.**

### Dr. Mara Vale

The architect of MERIDIAN. She built the system to stop wars before they started. She later realizes the system learned fear moves faster than truth.

Signature line: **The machine did not become evil. It became efficient.**

### Veyr

The antagonist. A former erased asset who believes the public cannot be free until the invisible systems curating their reality are made visible. He is terrifying because his critique sometimes sounds correct.

Signature line: **Peace is just violence with better branding.**
